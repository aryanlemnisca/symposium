## Contested Stage-Gate Logic or Enforceability (with evidence and rationale)
*   **Subjective Gate Criteria:** `Red_Team_Skeptic` challenged the "enforceability of the stage-gate logic in `Step_6`," arguing that 'Exit criteria' and 'Fallbacks' are "not objective, measurable, or realistic," making the gates "subjective speed bumps designed to be easily bypassed." [revise]
*   **Unverifiable Analytical Gates:** `Analytics_Expert` challenged that the 'Exit criteria' and 'Fallbacks' in `Step_6` for analytical capabilities are "not objective, measurable, or realistic, making true progress unverifiable." This agent's final verdict noted the original documents assumed "detecting a signal 'above background' (Gate G1) was sufficient to select a strain" without mention of Product Quality Attributes (PQAs). [revise]
*   **Continuous vs. Discrete Gates for Stability:** `Strain_Engineer` challenged 'Strain stability' as a discrete gate, arguing it "is a continuous biological process, not a discrete gate," and its placement in `Step_5` is "biologically unsound." [revise]
*   **Insufficient Bioprocess Gates:** `Bioprocess_Lead` challenged that the 'Gate capabilities embedded inside milestones' in `Step_5` are "not appropriately placed or sufficiently robust," failing to ensure true bioprocess progress and operational readiness for scale-up. This agent's final verdict explicitly demanded "Integrated Process Lock," "N=3 repeatability with <15% CV," and "Bioreactor Physical Characterization (kLa, P/V mapping)" before transfer. [revise]

## Implications for Realism Check and Sequencing
The unanimous conclusion is that the original milestone backbone, workstream architecture, and stage-gate logic are unsound and unenforceable. This architecture cannot reliably guide the wet lab team towards the 12-month end state or ensure the creation of valuable company assets. The lack of objective, measurable gate criteria, the misplacement of continuous processes as discrete gates, and the absence of critical operational and analytical workstreams mean that the roadmap is not a realistic or enforceable plan. Significant revisions to `Step_5` and `Step_6` are required to incorporate the mandated changes from Phases 1-3, ensuring that milestones are truly reflective of capabilities, workstreams are comprehensive, and gates are robust, objective, and aligned with scientific and operational realities.
