## Critical Issues to Fix Before Sequencing (prioritized, with document citations)
1.  **Biological & End State Credibility (`Step_1`):** The 12-month end state is "biologically impossible and commercially irrelevant" (`Strategy_Architect` synthesis). It assumes "20L scale-up like a simple volume change" and "a frozen upstream recipe was the same thing as a locked process" (`Bioprocess_Lead` final verdict), lacking "realistic biological development timelines, robust strain stability metrics, and iterative experimental design" (`Strain_Engineer` final verdict). [revise]
2.  **Operational Realism & Workstream Completeness (`Step_5`, `Step_6`):** The roadmap "assumed consumables teleport into the lab, operators don't need sleep, and 20L bioreactors clean themselves" (`Ops_Integrator` final verdict). It lacks critical operational workstreams, realistic resource allocation, and accurate lead times for equipment and facility modifications. [revise]
3.  **Analytical Rigor & Quality Control (`Step_3`, `Step_6`):** The pack "treated analytical chemistry as a passive, background 'enabler'" and assumed "detecting a signal 'above background' (Gate G1) was sufficient to select a strain... without a single mention of Product Quality Attributes (PQAs)" (`Analytics_Expert` final verdict). The scoring logic undervalues product quality, and gate criteria lack objective analytical measures. [revise]
4.  **Strategic Coherence & Logical Consistency (across `Step_0`-`Step_6`):** The roadmap is a "narrative-driven hallucination" and "masterpiece of project-management theater" (`Red_Team_Skeptic` final verdict), infected with "optimism bias." The overall logic is "incoherent and inconsistent" (`Strategy_Architect` synthesis), failing to align strategic objectives with technical and operational realities. [revise]
5.  **Enforceable Stage-Gate Logic (`Step_5`, `Step_6`):** The stage-gate logic is unsound and unenforceable, with "subjective, cowardly language" (`Red_Team_Skeptic` final verdict) in exit criteria, failure modes, and fallbacks. Gates are not objective decision points but "subjective speed bumps" (`Red_Team_Skeptic` challenge). [revise]
