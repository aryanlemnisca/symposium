## Strengths of Scope Selection Rationale (with evidence)
The four-tier categorization frame ('Visible end-state capabilities', 'Non-negotiable enabling foundations', 'Fit-for-purpose only', 'Defer') itself was implicitly accepted as a logical structure for roadmap planning by `Bioprocess_Lead` during synthesis, although its application was heavily contested. [accept]

## Contested Scope Categorizations (with evidence and rationale)
*   **Bioprocess Capabilities:** `Bioprocess_Lead` challenged that `Step_4` "hides the 'engine of production' by miscategorizing essential bioprocess capabilities as 'Fit-for-purpose' or 'Deferred,' which are non-negotiable for achieving repeatable 1L and 20L runs." [revise]
*   **Strategic Asset Building:** `Strategy_Architect` argued that the scope selection "strategically miscategorizes critical capabilities, particularly by deferring long-term asset-building elements," undermining market differentiation and investor appeal. Specifically, 'Platform reuse' was challenged for being 'Deferred' when it is "strategically critical... for long-term scalability and competitive advantage." [revise]
*   **Strain Stability:** `Ops_Integrator` initially challenged 'Strain stability' as a 'Visible end-state' but later conceded and defended its placement there, arguing it is a "visible outcome of a robust process" and a "critical proof point" for investors. [accept]

## Capabilities Wrongly Deferred or Over-Included (with evidence)
*   **Bioprocess Scale-up & Control:** `Bioprocess_Lead` challenged the "deferral of critical bioprocess scale-up and process control capabilities in `Step_4`," stating this "directly contradicts the need for repeatable 1L and 20L runs established in Phase 1." [revise]
*   **Analytical & Quality Control:** `Analytics_Expert` challenged the "deferral of 'Formulation / sample preparation' and 'Analytical method development' in `Step_4`," asserting these are "non-negotiable enabling foundations for accurate measurement and validation of the 12-month end state." [revise]
*   **Strain Engineering:** `Strain_Engineer` challenged that `Step_4` "wrongly defers critical early-stage strain engineering capabilities, such as advanced genetic tool development for novel chassis," which are "essential for achieving the 'fermenter-ready Yarrowia chassis' in 12 months." [revise]
*   **Operational Foundations:** `Ops_Integrator` challenged the "deferral of 'Equipment maintenance and calibration' in `Step_4`," identifying it as a "non-negotiable enabling foundation that directly impacts operational reliability and data integrity." This also extends to "supply chain, inventory, and equipment maintenance capabilities." [revise]

## Missing Justification for Key Scope Decisions
The discussion consistently highlighted a lack of sound justification for the categorization of numerous capabilities, particularly those placed in the 'Deferred' or 'Fit-for-purpose' categories. `Red_Team_Skeptic` explicitly noted that the "document's justifications for 'Deferred' items appear to contradict the urgent needs established in Phase 1 and 2," indicating a fundamental gap in the rationale provided in `Step_4`. The overall defensibility of the scope selection was challenged as being "narrative-driven" rather than based on rigorous logic.

## Implications for Milestone and Workstream Definition
The unanimous verdict that the scope selection in `Step_4` is indefensible has profound implications for subsequent roadmap steps. Any milestones or workstreams defined in `Step_5` and `Step_6` based on the original `Step_4` would be built upon a flawed foundation. The current scope misrepresents the true effort, dependencies, and strategic value of critical capabilities. Therefore, `Step_4` requires a complete overhaul to reclassify capabilities based on the resolutions from Phases 1 and 2, ensuring that the roadmap accurately reflects biological reality, operational feasibility, analytical rigor, and strategic asset building before defining any further milestones.
