# Lemnisca Triage — Build-Ready PRD

## 1. Product Name and One-Line Description
**Lemnisca Triage**: A mobile-first, 100% client-side diagnostic framework that translates 6:30 AM plant floor anomalies into QA-defensible risk mitigation rationales for the 8 AM standup.

## 2. Target User
  **Who exactly:** The frontline MSAT/Process Engineer who is the technical owner of the bioprocess.
  **In what situation:** It is 6:30 AM. They are receiving secondhand, panicked information from a night shift supervisor about a batch deviating from the golden trajectory, and they must present a credible framing to the Plant Manager, QA, and Ops at an 8:00 AM standup.
  **What they have available at that moment:** Only their mobile phone, under 60 seconds of time, and no ability to pull a full historian data dump.

## 3. Trigger Moment
  **What just happened that makes them open this:** A macroscopic alarm sounds on the plant floor (e.g., DO spike, base addition anomaly), standard SOPs have failed to resolve it, and Ops is demanding immediate instructions on what to do next.

## 4. Required Inputs
  [List each. Must be sparse — no historian integration, no API.]
  *   **Scale (Static Toggle):** "Pilot (<500L)" | "Commercial (>2000L)"
  *   **Phase (Static Toggle):** "Initial Batch" | "Exponential" | "Post-Induction/Production"
  *   **The Anomaly (Ranked Vertical List):** User selects the single highest-priority symptom occurring, strictly ordered by phenomenological hierarchy:
      1. Agitation Torque / Power Draw Anomaly
      2. Off-gas CO2 / CER Crash
      3. DO Crash / O2 Cascade Maxed Out
      4. Sudden DO Spike
      5. Unexplained Base/Acid Demand
      6. Massive Foaming Event
  *   **Immediate Preceding Action (Radio Buttons):** "Feed initiated" | "Sampling/Shift change" | "Temp/pH shift" | "None/Steady-state"

## 5. Processing Logic
  **What the tool computes or structures:** It runs a deterministic decision matrix (~35-40 pruned critical paths) to eliminate physically impossible scenarios and categorize the deviation into fundamental engineering regimes (e.g., transport limitation vs. kinetic failure). 
  **Mathematical or logical basis:** First-principles biochemical engineering, mass balances, and reaction kinetics (evaluating reaction rates vs. transport rates, Damköhler number shifts, and Kolmogorov microscales).
  **What it explicitly CANNOT infer:** It cannot perform Root Cause Analysis (RCA). It does not guess the exact mechanical bug or biological phage; it only defines the phenomenological bounding of the symptom.

## 6. Output
  **Exact format:** A 4-block structured summary:
      1. *Reality Check (Red Box):* Quickest physical way to verify the symptom and rule out mechanical failure.
      2. *The Physics (Grey Box):* The fundamental engineering regime failing (Kinetic vs. Mass Transfer).
      3. *Risk Mitigation Rationale (Yellow Box):* Standard phenomenological containment action, explicitly noting "MSAT to evaluate against current batch record."
      4. *Standup Prep (Blue Box):* 3 specific historian tags to pull before 8 AM.
  **What the user receives:** Plain, beautifully formatted Markdown text copied to their clipboard via a "Copy Structured Summary" button. The text includes bracketed blanks at the top (`[Incident Date: ___ ]`, `[Unit ID: ___ ]`, `[Batch Number: ___ ]`) and a Lemnisca watermark at the bottom.
  **How it is used or shared:** Pasted directly into Microsoft Teams, an electronic logbook, or a shift-handover PowerPoint the moment the user opens their laptop.

## 7. Trust Mechanism
  **Why an experienced engineer believes this output:** It relies strictly on the physical laws of cause and effect (transport phenomena dictating biological response) rather than black-box AI hallucinations. It explicitly prompts the engineer to rule out "Telephone Game" mechanical false alarms first.
  **Why it is defensible to QA:** It is explicitly framed as a *Decision Support & Investigation Scoping* artifact, not an automated controller. By using phrases like "Risk Mitigation Rationale" instead of "Containment Mandate," and by requiring the engineer to fill in bracketed blanks, it elevates human validation and complies with GMP environments where unvalidated software cannot dictate batch actions.
